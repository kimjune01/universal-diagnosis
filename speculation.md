# Speculation

**This is not investment advice.** These are thought experiments connecting the framework's structural predictions to hypothetical trading positions. The framework predicts process outcomes (will the company execute?), not stock prices.

## Where the framework has edge

The framework adds signal where it **disagrees** with consensus. Where it agrees (INMB bearish, SPRB bullish), there's no informational edge — the market already reflects the direction.

**ATYR is the edge case.** The framework predicts PASS (new sarcoidosis trial announced by September 30). Shkreli and the analyst community predict bear. The stock trades near $0.85, roughly at cash value. The market is pricing the Phase 3 miss as terminal. The framework says it isn't — the endpoint was wrong, not the drug, and the consolidate stack is functional enough to pivot.

If the framework is right, ATYR calls expiring after September 30 are cheap because the market hasn't priced in a functional consolidate stack. The May MHRA-style FDA Type C meeting is the leading indicator.

## Where the framework has no edge

**INMB** — we agree with Shkreli's direction (bear). No informational advantage over consensus. The structural prediction (MAA won't submit on time) may be correct, but the stock already reflects skepticism. If there's any residual hope priced into INMB calls, selling them captures time decay as September approaches without the MAA materializing. Going concern + cash burn makes theta work in our favor. Contingent on option liquidity at ~$1 — likely thin.

**SPRB** — we agree with Shkreli's direction (bull). The molecule carries the company. The framework adds nuance (execution fragility, meta-level trauma recurrence) but the direction is consensus.

## The vol hypothesis (untested)

If management systematically overframes ambiguous data, implied vol is calibrated to an overly narrow expectation. Any ambiguous catalyst produces a larger surprise than priced.

The candidates from this pilot:
- **ATYR** before the mid-April FDA Type C meeting — management will frame it optimistically regardless of content. If the meeting is ambiguous, the gap between framing and reality moves the stock more than IV implies.
- **INMB** before MHRA pre-MAA feedback (May 2026) — same pattern. Management will say it went well. If it didn't, surprise is underpriced.

This is a hypothesis. We haven't pulled historical IV vs realized vol data. The methodology is in the [blog post](https://june.kim/diagnosis-biotech). The backtest is future work.

## The simple reading

If you believe one thing from this project, believe this: **a company that can't read its own failures will repeat them.** That's the consolidate heuristic. QURE fought the FDA and lost. CAPR read the CRL and pivoted. Same regulatory system, opposite management responses, opposite outcomes. The framework catches the difference. Most analysts don't look.
